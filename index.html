HORMONE HACKING

Growth plates, thin layers of cartilage at the end of bones, are crucial for growth and development. They are often associated with the belief that closed growth plates prevent taller individuals from growing. However, recent scientific evidence suggests that growth can occur at any age, possibly due to the fact that growth plates never close. This understanding of growth plates is essential for understanding their function and potential benefits in promoting physical growth.

CARTILAGE AND IT'S OSSIFICATION

The cartilage in your growth plates is hyaline cartilage, unlike the elastic cartilage in your ears. Growth plates are responsible for most bone growth in children and teens through a process called endochondral ossification. In this process, cartilage cells (chondrocytes) in the epiphyseal plates gradually turn into bone cells. During growth spurts, as cartilage grows, it turns into bone until the entire cartilage is ossified.

ARTICULAR CARTILAGE

Stimuli: Your growth plates never fully close because articular cartilage remains open. To trigger growth, articular chondrocytes must change into epiphyseal chondrocytes, which happens through stimuli. There are two types: mechanical and chemical.

Mechanical Stimuli:Mechanical stimuli involve forces transferred to the bone, such as landing on your feet, muscle force during activities like arm wrestling, or a combination like sprinting. The exercises should generate tensional force (which stimulates bone lengthening) rather than compressive force (which makes bones wider).

Chemical Stimuli: Chemical stimuli refer to natural chemicals in your body, such as hormones, growth factors, and proteins. The most crucial are the hormone HGH and the growth factor IGF-1.
